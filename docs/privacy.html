GLOBAL PULSE — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 1, 2026

Global Pulse is a screensaver that displays a live feed of public world events on your TV. We designed it to collect as little as possible.

What we collect

Nothing personal. Global Pulse does not require an account, does not ask you to sign in, and does not collect, store, or share any personally identifiable information. There is no advertising and no third-party tracking in the app.

How it works

To show live events, the app requests a public, read-only feed of world events (news headlines, earthquakes, natural events, severe weather, and rocket launches) from our service at global-pulse-two.vercel.app. It also streams a globe animation and images hosted on a public content delivery network, and event headline images are loaded from their original news sources.

These requests are standard internet requests required to display content. We do not associate them with you, and we do not build a profile of you.

Device and platform data

Roku may collect device and usage information under Roku's own privacy policy as part of operating your device and the Roku platform. That collection is governed by Roku, not by Global Pulse. See Roku's privacy policy for details.

Children

Global Pulse is a general-audience screensaver and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children.

Changes

If this policy changes, we'll update the date above and post the new version at this URL.
